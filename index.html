My favorite Genres

Survival Horror

Survival Horror focuses on tight corrodors, limited supplies, and tense encounters with monsters. Critically you're given options to fight back against whatever creepy monsters are chasing you, a key difference from standard horror games.

RPG

Role-Playing Games focus on a character you create, and regardless if the setting is fantasy, sci-fi, or a mix between, you are given the starring role and make decisions that alter the world around you.

Shooter

Shooters are the most straight forward genre, they involve shooting some kind of weapon. Whether it be a gun, a magic staff, or something else. They often take a first or third person perspective.

Massively Multiplayer Online RPG

MMO RPGs combine the characteristics of the RPG genre and an online community where you can do these things alongside other players. Other activities involve gathering friends together to fight massive battles against other players or challenging bosses, and contain a wealth of other life activites as well such as cooking, farming, and more!

Action

Action games are typically focused around controlling a character that is granted a suite of different abilities, spells, and special attacks that are often able to be configured to behave differently to suit your tastes, or continously upgraded to be more bombastic and entertaining to watch. There are a variety of camera styles that action games take place in, and often are constrained to "levels" with limited exploration available.

Action/Adventure

An Action/Adventure game has all the properties of an Action game, but instead of feeling constrained and not being able to explore a larger world, these games allow you to explore massive areas to your hearts content and discover breathtaking sites and secret, challenging fights!

Best in their Genre

While shown in the genres above as examples, the purpose was actually two fold: Not only to provide an example of what each genre is, but to provide stellar games that are top shelf examples of what each genre wants to accomplish. Here I'll go further in depth about these games.

Nioh 3

Action/Adventure

Nioh 3 is a historical fiction piece set in Japan, it features time travel that allows you to visit different Eras in Japans' History, such as the Edo Period, the Sengoku Period, the Heian Period, and the Bakamatsu Period. You get to create your own original character and explore through Japan in all these wildly different eras, interacting with historical figures and fighting against both famous and original Yokai. The game's very action heavy and quite the tough cookie to beat as well!

Path of Exile 2

Action

Path of Exile 2 is one of the more unique action games, as it features a camera looking at both the environment and your character in a top-down PoV. You get to choose one of many archetypes of warriors such as witchs, barbarians, druids, magicians and more against a wild variety of monsters in a dark fantasy setting. The hallmark of the game and what makes it so special is just how modular all your magic spells and abilities are, allowing you to create a character that is entirely unique from every other person who's played the game.

Marvel Rivals

Shooter

Shooters can cover a variety of settings, from fantasy to sci-fi to cyberpunk, and you can name stellar examples from each of these. However I think that the game that nailed how fun the genre can be is Marvel Rivals. Being able to play as my favorite heroes is amazing, and they often feel very real to their comic versions, and being able to feel as powerful as Magneto or Cyclops is a dream come true.

Silent Hill f

Survival Horror

Survival Horror thrives in making you feel uncomfortable as often as it can through any method, and the Silent Hill franchise is an exemplary example of this. Silent Hill does an amazing job at setting a tense atmosphere and keeping the string taut constantly, to the point that having a weapon to defend yourself and that much of your time is in daylight does nothing to dissipate that tension. The story takes place in early 1900s Japan, where the households were deeply patriarchal and the rights of women and their standing in society was trodden down upon.
There's many times where the game takes a hard look at how bad that society was and the horror found living through it as a girl. A hallmark of the franchise is that the monster design is often drawn from the main characters traumas or mental struggles, and there is many times when a monster will look like a disfigured school girl or its implied it reflects sexual assault. Playing through the game, the story outside of these subliminal messages is incredibly intriguing as you try to escape the main characters town that is covered in an oppressive fog that holds these monsters. Famously it's written by renowned Psychological Horror author "Ryukishi07", who uses his background as a social worker and the horrors of child abuse and neglect he witnessed to construct chilling stories focusing on these aspects.

Final Fantasy XIV Online

MMO

Final Fantasy XIV is a celebration of the franchise, containing elements of every single other game in it's lengthy history. It does this while not failing to construct its own identity both narratively and through gameplay. It showcasing visually stunning effects when casting spells and other abilities, and the story has been running concurrently for over a decade, and being part of that journey is a treat of the highest order. There's also plenty of side activities to get lost in like searching for elusive fish or playing mini games in casinos, the game is bursting with activities to do that even if you've played every day for years you wouldn't get tired of.

WarHammer 40k: Rogue Trader

RPG

An RPG taking place in the grimdark universe of 40k ended up being a match made in heaven. Typically a character living in the 40k universe wouldn't be allowed to live very quickly into the story, but as a "Rogue Trader", your character gets a free pass to commit heresy, or what the imperium views as heresy(which is most things), as much as they want. Rogue Traders are tasked with exploring, trading, and expanding into the unexplored reaches of space.
This allows us to get to interact with many different aspects of the 40k universe, and indulge in a power fantasy of being the most important person in the room everytime you walk into one. It also contains a vast wealth of story which prompts you to make decisions that will decide to fate of billions of people very frequently, and you get to lament that in this grimdark world trying to bring empathy into it just gets your laughed out of the room or shot at.
