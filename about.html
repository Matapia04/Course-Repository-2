Why I love Gaming

This page will function as a blog and way for me to gush over how much I love the gaming medium. I think games are such an amazing thing that give the opportunity to both explore entirely new worlds and the worlds of existing IPs. The feeling of exploring uniquely crafted worlds with surprises waiting around every corner is second to none, and the stories these games tell, whether it being an modern action hero or starring in a whimsical fantasy adventure can pull on the heart strings in every way possible.

Not to mention that I've made many friends playing games, whether it be while out and about and running into someone and finding out we share that common interest and passion or in the game itself. Finding friends while playing the game is always a treat, and builds a great sense of community within the game space where we can comfortably share experiences and advice with each other, building strong bonds that, for me, have lasted years!

Favorite Genres

The following are my personal favorite game genres

  • RPGs

    • I really enjoy being able to create a character and be the centerpiece of the story, with my decisions having meaningful impact on the world and characters in it.

  • Survival Horror

    Survival Horror isn't as spooky as regular horror, because you have a gun. And that makes the game more fun and sometimes even scarier because I have a gun but it does nothing to reassure me

  • Action

    • Action games have a huge variety of options to choose from, with differing camera perspectives and ways that action takes place, it could be slinging around magic or having an amarda of swords and other weapons at your disposal. They're all amazing

  • Shooter

    • Being a cool military action guy or super hero and competing against other players in online matches is incredibly addicting and fun!

Top 5 games all time

This isn't in any order

Kingdom Hearts 2

Kingdom Hearts 2 is a game that made up many of my core memories in my childhood. Everyone I knew growing up had played this game, and I vividly remember my older brother helping me beat the harder parts of the game. The entire franchise is based on a wacky premise of disney characters and final fantasy characters being smashed into the same universe, but Donald Duck being a grumpy mage is the best thing ever. It has a lot of really cool moments and tweaks to the stories of classic Disney movies like Pirates of the Caribbean, and the gameplay is on the simpler side but it's a joy to play through every single time.

Marvel Rivals

As an avid comic book fan, Marvel Rivals is a dream come true in many ways.
I've always loved the personal stories characters went through during their adventures, but in movies and shows they either changed the story or never got around to adapting it. But Marvel Rivals lovingly pulls characters right out of the comics, all of their relationships and powers and stories intact.
Being able to hear two characters I know and love talk to each other about things that happened in a comic on my shelf is incredibly heartwarming to know that the people who made this game also have that same comic on their shelf as well. It doesn't just extend to their character voicelines either, all of their powers are transferred right into game form and I get to feel what it's like to be The Hulk, Wolverine, Magik, and more. The chaotic fights I get into the game, making me think I'm in a genuine Marvel Hero fight is just the cherry on top.

Final Fantasy XIV

Final Fantasy 14 will forever have a very special place in my heart, as during one of the lowest points in my life, playing this game helped a lot. It was when my father passed, and naturally my Mother and I were destroyed by it, and during that time I started playing games a lot more, especially this one. While doing so there were often really cinematic and exciting moments that would happen in the story or during regular adventures I had while playing, and I began dragging my Mom over to watch. During these frequent sessions she barely understood what was happening but still indulged me and we would talk about whatever I was doing, and later about my Dad. It helped ease us into that process of grief and strengthened our bond much more.

The game itself is a wonderful playground for exploring the fantastical world, fighting unique monsters, playing as a variety of different classes like warrior, mage, bard, and more, and enjoying the incredible saga the story tells, and is still telling for over a decade.

God of War & God of War:Ragnarok

I cheated and included two in one! It's hard to seperate these two because the story is so tightly woven it feels like naming half a game if i just named only one.
God of War and it's sequel are actually sequels to an older trilogy of the same name that were released over a decade ago. In those games you follow Kratos, the God of War, as he wages war against the Greek pantheon for the crimes they committed against him.

In his rage he ends up killing most of the Greek Gods, and years later we discover he had moved on to the Norse mythology to live a quiet life, overcome with shame over what he did prior. These two games focus on his growth as he tries to move past what he used to be, and a major motivation for that is that in these games he has a son who he wants to become a better man for. The gameplay itself plays almost like you're watching a movie with how cinematic it is, and you can do all sorts of cool techniques using magical blades, spears, and his trusty axe. The message being told is also wonderful as well, as we should all move forward into being better then we were yesterday!

Resident Evil 4

Resident Evil 4 is part of the long and storied surviival horro franchise Resident Evil, and I'm specifically referring to the remake that came out a couple years ago. It is a hallmark of what makes the genre so incredible, though it does lean more towards an action movie style. Especially since the premise is that you're part of a government agency on a mission to rescue the president's daughter from an evil cult. The evil cult turns people into monsters and is who you are facing the entire time, using conventional modern weaponry like hand guns, snipers, shotguns, and more.

You play as Leon S. Kennedy, the coolest man alive. As he does unnecessary back flips to dodge attacks, suplexes the evil cult members, and kicks them in the head so hard they explode. It's very silly, but you still have the traditional gameplay of survival horror where more grotesque monster/human hybrids attack you, and ammo is scarce so all the acrobatics are necessary!
Especially later on, and the game does get very scary when you encounter a monster that will relentlessly pursue you and regenerate any damage that's done to it. In the end this combination of over the top action and tense horror elements make the game never get old, and I've replayed it countless times.
